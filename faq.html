FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

A quick explanation of what Japan Life Explained is, how to use it, and what it cannot replace.

Quick answer

Use this site to understand the basic idea and common steps. Then check official sources or ask the relevant office, company, school, clinic, bank, or real estate agency before making important decisions.

Is this an official Japanese government website?

No. Japan Life Explained is an independent guide site. It explains common procedures in plain English, but official offices and professional advisers should be used for final decisions.

Can I rely on this site for legal, tax, medical, or immigration advice?

No. This site is a general starting point. Rules can differ by city, employer, school, visa status, bank, clinic, and personal situation.

Who is this site for?

It is for people who are working, living, studying, or preparing to live in Japan, especially people who find official Japanese procedures difficult to understand.

Why do the guides include Japanese and romaji?

Because many forms, counters, payslips, clinic documents, and rental papers use Japanese terms. Romaji helps readers say or recognize the words.

Why are the articles simple?

The goal is not to cover every exception. The goal is to help readers understand the basic idea, common mistakes, and next step before checking official details.

What should I do if my situation is different?

Check official sources and ask the relevant office, company, school, employer, clinic, bank, or real estate agency directly.

Can I request a new topic?

Yes. Use the Request a topic page to suggest topics that would help foreign residents in Japan.

Why does the site use cat tips?

The cat tips are small reminders that make stressful procedures feel a little less intimidating.

🐱 Cat tip: If a procedure feels confusing, first identify the office, document, deadline, and next action. That alone makes it less scary.
