About this site

About Japan Life Explained

Japan Life Explained is a simple guide site for people who are working, living, or preparing to live in Japan.

What this site does

This site explains everyday systems in Japan in plain English: salary, taxes, health insurance, clinics, address registration, renting, banking, phones, and other practical topics.

The goal is simple: make confusing Japanese procedures easier to understand before you are standing at a counter, holding a form, and wondering what to do next.

Who this site is for

New residents

People who recently moved to Japan and need to handle city office, health insurance, banking, phone, and housing procedures.

Workers in Japan

People who want to understand payslips, take-home pay, resident tax, social insurance, and salary deductions.

Students and job seekers

People preparing for life in Japan and trying to understand what documents and procedures may come next.

People helping foreign residents

Employers, friends, family members, and support staff who want simple English explanations to share.

How the guides are written

Plain English first
The guides avoid unnecessary legal or bureaucratic language whenever possible.
Japanese words included
Important terms are shown in Japanese, romaji, and English so you can recognize them on forms or signs.
Practical flow
Each guide focuses on what people actually need to do, bring, ask, or check.
Common misunderstandings
Many problems come from assumptions, so each guide highlights easy-to-miss points.

What this site is not

This site is not a government website, law office, tax office, immigration office, city office, hospital, bank, or real estate agency. It does not replace official instructions or professional advice.

Rules can differ depending on your visa status, city, employer, school, bank, phone company, clinic, landlord, and personal situation. Always check official sources and ask the relevant office or company when making important decisions.

Why the site uses “simple guides”

Many official pages are accurate but hard to read. Many personal blogs are easy to read but may be outdated or based on one person’s experience. Japan Life Explained tries to sit between them: friendly enough to understand, careful enough to avoid pretending that every case is the same.
