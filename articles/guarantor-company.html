Guarantor Companies in Japan: What Renters Should Know

When renting an apartment in Japan, you may be asked to use a guarantor company. This can be confusing because it feels like another fee on top of rent, deposit, and key money.

Quick answer

A guarantor company is a company that helps guarantee rent payment to the landlord. Many rental contracts in Japan require one, even if you have a job or enough savings. The tenant usually pays a guarantor fee at the start and may also pay renewal fees.

Why guarantor companies are used

Landlords want confidence that rent will be paid. In the past, many contracts required a personal guarantor, often a family member. Now many properties use guarantor companies instead.

For foreign renters, this can be especially common because landlords may worry about language, visa status, length of stay, overseas emergency contacts, or whether rent can be collected if there is a problem.

What the fee usually means

The guarantor fee is not rent and is usually not a deposit. It is a fee paid to the guarantor company for providing the guarantee service. The fee structure depends on the company and property.

Initial guarantor fee

A fee paid when signing the contract. It may be based on rent or a fixed amount.

Renewal fee

Some guarantor companies charge a renewal fee every year or at contract renewal.

Monthly fee

Some contracts include a monthly guarantor or payment service fee.

Late payment cost

If rent is late, extra fees or collection procedures may apply. Check the contract carefully.

What foreign renters should check

  • Is a guarantor company required?
  • How much is the initial guarantor fee?
  • Is there a yearly renewal fee?
  • Is there a monthly guarantor or payment service fee?
  • What documents are needed for screening?
  • Is an emergency contact required in Japan?
  • What happens if rent payment is late?
  • Is the fee refundable? Usually it is not, but check the contract.

Common misunderstandings

“A guarantor company is the same as a deposit.”

No. A deposit may be used for unpaid rent or damage. A guarantor fee is usually a service fee and may not be refunded.

“If I use a guarantor company, I do not need to pay rent carefully.”

No. You are still responsible for rent. The guarantor company may seek payment from you if it covers unpaid rent.

“All properties accept foreigners if I pay the fee.”

No. Some properties may still have screening conditions, document requirements, or landlord preferences.

“The cheapest guarantor fee is always best.”

Not always. Check renewal fees, monthly fees, and payment rules too.

How this connects to move-in costs

The guarantor company fee is usually part of the move-in cost breakdown. When comparing apartments, do not look only at monthly rent. Add the guarantor fee, renewal fee, insurance, agency fee, deposit, key money, and cleaning costs to understand the real cost.

🐱 Cat tip: If you see 保証料 on a rental estimate, ask whether it is one-time, yearly, or monthly. Tiny wording can change the total cost.

Summary

Guarantor companies are common in Japanese rental contracts. They reduce risk for landlords but add costs for tenants. Before signing, check the initial fee, renewal fee, monthly fee, screening requirements, emergency contact rules, and what happens if payment is late.
