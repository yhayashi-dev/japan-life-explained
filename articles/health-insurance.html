Health Insurance in Japan: What New Residents Should Know

Japan has public health insurance systems. If you live in Japan, you usually need to be enrolled in a health insurance system and show your insurance card or eligibility confirmation when visiting a clinic.

Quick answer

Health insurance in Japan usually reduces the amount you pay at clinics and hospitals. Many working employees are covered through their employer. People who are not covered by employer insurance usually join National Health Insurance through their city or ward office.

Two common types

Employee health insurance

Many full-time employees and some part-time employees are enrolled through their employer. The insurance premium is usually deducted from salary.

National Health Insurance

People not covered by employee insurance often enroll at the city or ward office. Premiums are usually paid to the municipality.

What insurance usually does

When you use public health insurance at a covered clinic or hospital, you usually pay only part of the medical cost at the counter. The exact share can depend on age, income, and situation.

Health insurance does not mean everything is free. You still usually pay a portion of the cost, and some services may not be covered.

Insurance card, My Number card, and eligibility

Japan is changing how people show health insurance eligibility. Some people use a My Number card registered for health insurance, while others may use other confirmation documents depending on their situation.

🐱 Cat tip: When visiting a clinic, bring your Residence Card and any health insurance proof you have, especially if you are not sure what the clinic accepts.

Common misunderstandings

“Health insurance means medical care is free.”

No. Insurance usually reduces your payment, but you often still pay part of the cost.

“My employer always handles everything.”

Not always. It depends on your employment status and whether you are enrolled in employee insurance.

“I can wait until I get sick to join.”

That can create problems. If you live in Japan, insurance enrollment is usually expected.

“All treatments are covered.”

No. Some treatments, documents, vaccines, cosmetic procedures, or special services may not be covered.

What to prepare

  • Residence Card
  • Address registration information
  • Passport, if requested
  • Employment information, if joining through work
  • Any insurance card, My Number card, or eligibility document you have
  • Payment method for premiums or clinic fees

When to ask your employer or city office

If you are employed, ask your employer whether you are enrolled in employee health insurance. If you are not covered through work, ask your city or ward office about National Health Insurance. Do not guess based only on your job title.

Summary

Health insurance is one of the most important systems for living in Japan. New residents should understand whether they are covered through an employer or need to enroll at the city or ward office. Bring your insurance proof when visiting clinics and remember that insurance usually reduces costs, not eliminates them.
