How to Read a Japanese Payslip

A Japanese payslip can look intimidating because it contains many short words and deduction names. Start by separating it into earnings, deductions, and final payment.

Quick answer

To read a Japanese payslip, first find the total earnings, then find the total deductions, and finally find the amount actually paid. The final payment may be written as 差引支給額 or another similar term depending on the company system.

The three main areas

1. Earnings area
This shows salary, overtime pay, commuting allowance, and other payments.
2. Deductions area
This shows taxes, health insurance, pension, employment insurance, and other deductions.
3. Final payment area
This is the amount transferred to your bank account.

Common earnings items

Basic salary

Your regular base salary before overtime or extra allowances.

Overtime pay

Additional pay for overtime work, if applicable.

Commuting allowance

Money paid for commuting costs. It may appear as a separate item.

Other allowances

Depending on the company, there may be housing, family, qualification, or position allowances.

Common deduction items

所得税

Income tax withheld from your salary.

住民税

Resident tax. It may start later depending on your situation.

健康保険

Health insurance deduction.

厚生年金

Employees' pension insurance deduction.

雇用保険

Employment insurance deduction.

その他控除

Other deductions, such as company-specific fees, if applicable.

How to check your payslip step by step

Step 1: Find total earnings
Look for words like 総支給額 or 支給合計.
Step 2: Find total deductions
Look for 控除合計 or the list of tax and insurance deductions.
Step 3: Find final payment
Look for 差引支給額, 振込額, or a similar final amount.
Step 4: Compare with your bank deposit
The final payment should usually match the amount transferred to your bank account.

Common misunderstandings

“All numbers are money I will receive.”

No. Some numbers are earnings, and some are deductions.

“The largest number is my take-home pay.”

Not always. The largest number may be total earnings before deductions.

“Resident tax missing means it will never be deducted.”

No. It may start later depending on your previous-year income and municipality.

“If I cannot read everything, I cannot understand anything.”

You can still understand the basics by finding total earnings, total deductions, and final payment.

Useful phrases

🐱 Cat tip: Do not try to read the whole payslip at once. First find three things: earnings, deductions, and final payment.

Summary

A Japanese payslip becomes easier when you divide it into three parts: earnings, deductions, and final payment. The most important number for your bank account is usually 差引支給額 or 振込額. If you are unsure, ask your company which item is the actual transfer amount.
