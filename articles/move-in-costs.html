Move-in Costs in Japan: Key Money, Deposit, and Fees

Renting an apartment in Japan can require several upfront payments. The first bill may be much higher than one month of rent.

Quick answer

Move-in costs in Japan may include deposit, key money, agency fee, guarantor company fee, first month rent, next month rent, insurance, lock exchange fee, cleaning fee, and other contract-related costs. Always ask for a written breakdown before signing.

Why the first payment is high

In many countries, people expect to pay only rent and a deposit. In Japan, the initial payment can include several different fees, and some of them are not returned. This is why the first payment can feel surprisingly high.

🐱 Cat tip: Do not compare only monthly rent. Compare the total initial cost and the renewal cost too.

Common move-in cost items

Deposit

Money kept as security for unpaid rent or damage. Some or all may be returned depending on the contract and move-out condition.

Key money

A non-refundable payment to the landlord. It is common in some rentals but not all.

Agency fee

A fee paid to the real estate agency for arranging the rental contract.

Guarantor company fee

A fee for a company that guarantees payment if the tenant does not pay rent.

First month / next month rent

You may pay rent for the current month and the next month upfront.

Insurance and other fees

Fire insurance, lock exchange, cleaning, support service, and other fees may appear.

Because of these items, an apartment with affordable monthly rent can still require a large upfront payment.

Common misunderstandings

“Deposit and key money are the same.”

No. Deposit may be used for unpaid rent or damage and may be partly returned. Key money is usually not returned.

“The cheapest rent is always the cheapest apartment.”

Not always. Initial costs, renewal fees, and move-out costs can change the total cost.

“The real estate agency owns the apartment.”

Usually no. The agency often introduces the property and handles paperwork, but the landlord owns it.

“All fees are negotiable.”

Some may be negotiable, some may not. Ask politely and get the final breakdown in writing.

What to check before signing

  • Total initial payment
  • Deposit and whether it can be returned
  • Key money
  • Agency fee
  • Guarantor company fee and renewal fee
  • Fire insurance cost
  • Lock exchange and cleaning fees
  • Contract period and renewal fee
  • Move-out cleaning and restoration rules

Summary

Move-in costs in Japan are often more than one month of rent. The most important thing is to ask for a written breakdown and understand which costs are refundable, non-refundable, one-time, or recurring. Compare total cost, not only monthly rent.
