How to Register Your Address in Japan

After moving to Japan or moving within Japan, you may need to register your address at your city or ward office. This is one of the first important steps for daily life.

Quick answer

Address registration connects your legal address to your Residence Card and local municipality. It can affect health insurance, resident tax, My Number, bank accounts, phone contracts, and official mail. Go to the city or ward office that handles your new address and complete the required moving procedure.

Why address registration matters

Your registered address is used for many everyday systems in Japan. If your address is not registered or is outdated, other procedures may become difficult.

  • Opening or updating a bank account
  • Signing a phone contract
  • Receiving official mail
  • Health insurance and pension procedures
  • Resident tax procedures
  • My Number-related documents
  • School, employer, or immigration-related paperwork

Common situations

Moving into Japan

If you newly arrive in Japan and have an address, you usually register it at the local city or ward office.

Moving within the same city or ward

You may need to submit a moving notice for the new address within the same municipality.

Moving to another city or ward

You may need procedures at both the old and new municipalities, such as moving-out and moving-in notices.

Leaving Japan

You may need to notify the municipality before leaving, especially if ending your residence in Japan.

Typical moving procedures

Moving in
Submit a moving-in notice at the city or ward office for your new address.
Moving out
If moving to another municipality, you may need a moving-out procedure at your old office.
Address change
If moving within the same municipality, the procedure may be called an address change or moving within the city.
Residence Card update
The new address may be written or updated on your Residence Card.

What to bring

  • Residence Card
  • Passport, if requested
  • My Number card or notification document, if you have one
  • Moving-out certificate, if you moved from another municipality and received one
  • New address information
  • Documents for family members, if registering together

Requirements can differ by municipality, so check the official website of your city or ward office before going.

Common misunderstandings

“My apartment contract is enough.”

No. A rental contract and address registration are different things. You may still need to register at the city or ward office.

“Changing the address with my company updates everything.”

No. Your employer, bank, phone company, and municipality are separate systems.

“The city office knows automatically when I move.”

Usually no. You often need to submit the correct moving notice.

“I can do it anywhere.”

No. You usually need the office responsible for your address or old address, depending on the procedure.

After registration

After your address is registered, check whether you also need to update your address with other places.

  • Employer or school
  • Bank
  • Mobile phone company
  • Credit card company
  • Health insurance or pension office, if applicable
  • Delivery services and online shopping accounts
🐱 Cat tip: Address registration is boring, but it unlocks many other procedures. Do it early.

Summary

Address registration is one of the most important first steps after moving in Japan. It links your address to municipal records and often affects banks, phones, insurance, taxes, and official mail. Bring your Residence Card and check your municipality’s instructions before going.
